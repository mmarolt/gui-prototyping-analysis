Fast Facts

Coca-Cola 101

+

Coca-Cola was first introduced in 1886 by pharmacist John Pemberton in Atlanta, Georgia. It was initially marketed as a patent medicine.

Today, The Coca-Cola Company offers more than 500 brands in over 200 countries, serving 1.9 billion drinks daily.

Products & Ingredients

+

Our portfolio includes sparkling soft drinks, water, sports drinks, juices, dairy, plant-based beverages, and coffee.

Key ingredients in Coca-Cola include carbonated water, high fructose corn syrup (or sugar), caramel color, phosphoric acid, natural flavors, and caffeine.

Global Impact

+

Coca-Cola's global operations support millions of jobs directly and indirectly through its vast supply chain and distribution network.

We are committed to various sustainability initiatives, including water stewardship, packaging recycling, and climate action.

FAQ

Well-being

+

Q: Are there sugar-free options available?

A: Yes, we offer a wide range of no-sugar and low-sugar beverages, including Coca-Cola Zero Sugar, Diet Coke, and various other light options across our brands.

Q: What is the caffeine content in your drinks?

A: Caffeine content varies by product. For example, a standard 12 fl oz (355 ml) can of Coca-Cola contains about 34 mg of caffeine, while Diet Coke has 46 mg.

Environment

+

Q: What are your sustainability goals?

A: We aim to collect and recycle the equivalent of every bottle or can we sell by 2030, and to make our packaging 100% recyclable. We also focus on water stewardship and reducing our carbon footprint.

Q: How can I recycle your packaging?

A: Most of our packaging, including PET plastic bottles, aluminum cans, and glass bottles, is widely recyclable. Please check local recycling guidelines in your area for proper disposal.
